Work & Research

Sep 2024 – Feb 2025
NIT Karnataka
Junior Research Fellow
  • Steering Actuator — System Design: Designed the complete electromechanical steering actuator assembly for a Mahindra REVA autonomous campus shuttle. Defined system architecture: NEMA-23 bipolar stepper motor (45 kgcm holding torque), 1:3 spur gear reduction delivering 11 Nm at the steering column, and custom-fabricated mild steel enclosure (242×174×128 mm, IP65 rated).
  • Component Selection & BOM: Specified and sourced all hardware — DM860H microstepping driver (7.2A, 24–110V), STM32 NUCLEO-G474RE controller, incremental rotary encoder (1000 PPR, 0.09° resolution), TJA1051 CAN transceiver, Deutsch DT series waterproof connectors, and 6204-2RS sealed bearings. Assembled and verified 22 AWG shielded automotive-grade wiring harness.
  • PID Control Implementation: Implemented closed-loop angular position control in Arduino/C++. Tuned PID gains with acceleration limiting for smooth directional response; encoder feedback at 4000 counts/revolution. Validated steering angle tracking before integration handoff.
  • Electrical Integration: Wired motor, driver, encoder, and STM32. Established CAN 2.0B communication at 1 Mbps with 120Ω termination. Configured microstepping at 1600 steps/rev; verified startup diagnostics and encoder direction alignment.
  • Structural Analysis & Fabrication: Performed ANSYS structural analysis to verify enclosure and mounting integrity. Coordinated chassis modifications and component fitment on the REVA platform including differential drive hardware and swing arm integration.
  • Testing & Documentation: Conducted hardware-in-the-loop (HIL) validation of steering response. Documented full assembly procedure, torque specifications, and calibration steps. Verified encoder shaft concentricity within 0.05 mm and mechanical clearances throughout.
